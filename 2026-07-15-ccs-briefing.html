UK's Airhive acquires Dutch rival Carbyon to form a larger European DAC company

Bloomberg · 14 Jul 2026 · [Europe & UK] · [paywall]

London-based direct-air-capture startup Airhive has acquired Dutch competitor Carbyon (terms undisclosed) in a deal it says creates one of Europe's biggest integrated DAC companies. The two bring complementary solid-sorbent approaches — Airhive's fluidised-bed technology and Carbyon's patented high-kinetics sorbent — and will operate cross-border under the Airhive name, concentrating early R&D at High Tech Campus Eindhoven on a next-generation "Cascade" system aimed at lower-cost capture. Airhive framed the move as consolidation as DAC demand and policy support turn less predictable just as the sector approaches commercialisation — a notable market-structure signal in engineered carbon removal. Microsoft's emissions jump 25% on AI buildout as it reaffirms carbon-removal commitment

Carbon Pulse · 14 Jul 2026 · [North America] · [paywall]

Microsoft's 2026 sustainability report shows FY2025 greenhouse-gas emissions of about 20 Mt CO₂e — up 25% year-on-year and roughly 58% above its 2020 baseline — driven by AI data-centre expansion. The company reaffirmed its carbon-negative-by-2030 goal and continued to expand one of the world's largest corporate CO₂-removal portfolios (29 projects added across 10 removal pathways; more than 45 Mt of removals contracted over three decades, including a 650 kt BECCS offtake with BioCirc), even as its reported H1-2026 offtake volume fell 82% to ~9.5 Mt. As the single largest corporate buyer of engineered CDR and BECCS credits, Microsoft's rising abatement gap — and its offtake volatility — is a first-order demand signal for the CCS/BECCS credit market.
