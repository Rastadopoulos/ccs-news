ExxonMobil surrenders 850,000 acres of Gulf of Mexico federal CO₂-storage leases

Carbon Herald / Energy Intelligence · 3 Jul 2026 · [North America]

ExxonMobil is letting go of 163 federal shallow-water leases spanning roughly 850,000 acres in the Gulf of Mexico that it picked up in the 2021 and 2023 BOEM lease sales for under US$25 million — tracts widely seen as marginal for hydrocarbons but premium, highly prospective real estate for large-scale sub-seabed CO₂ sequestration. With several relinquishments already accepted by federal authorities, the supermajor is redirecting capital toward onshore and Texas state-water storage, where it already holds the largest US offshore CO₂ lease (~271,000 acres with the Texas General Land Office) and where permitting and revenue are closer at hand. Energy Intelligence links the retreat to the Interior Department shelving a rule that would have let legacy oil-and-gas leases convert into dedicated CO₂-storage blocks — a framework ExxonMobil had lobbied hard for. The move is a pointed signal of how federal permitting uncertainty is steering US CCS investment toward states that hold Class VI primacy.

Still on the radar

Ucaneo inaugurates Germany’s largest direct air capture plant in Berlin

Carbon Herald / Siemens · 2 Jul 2026 · [Europe & UK]

Berlin-based Ucaneo opened Germany’s largest DAC facility in Berlin-Marzahn — a fully electrified electrochemical unit rated at 150 tonnes CO₂/yr delivering >99.9% purity, with part of the captured CO₂ bound for permanent geological storage, making it Germany’s first verified DAC-plus-storage project. Siemens joined as automation and digitalisation partner; a follow-on commercial plant with roughly ten times the capacity is slated to start construction next year.

Iowa regulators strip Dakotas conditions from Summit Carbon pipeline permit

Carbon Herald / Iowa Capital Dispatch · 2 Jul 2026 · [North America]

The Iowa Utilities Commission issued two orders removing language that had tied Summit Carbon Solutions’ ~US$8bn, 688-mile CO₂ pipeline permit to regulatory approvals in North and South Dakota, instead requiring Summit to demonstrate access to sequestration sites. The change potentially clears the way for alternative routing and storage after South Dakota’s permit denial and its ban on eminent domain for CO₂ pipelines.

PortNews · 29 Jun 2026 · [Australia & APAC] · via Shelly Murrell weekly monitoring
