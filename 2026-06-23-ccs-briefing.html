EU leaders' draft summit conclusions: extra free ETS allowances for heavy industry from January 2026

ESG News · 22 Jun 2026 · [EU]

Brussels is preparing to allocate a larger share of free EU ETS allowances to chemicals, refining and other CO₂-intensive sectors, with changes applying from 1 January 2026. Per draft Council conclusions circulated ahead of the EU leaders' summit, free allowances would cover roughly 75% of industrial emissions on average, saving in-scope companies an estimated €4 bn in CO₂ costs over the next few years. The proposal responds to pressure from Italy, Poland and the Czech Republic on competitiveness grounds and precedes the European Commission's formal ETS revision proposal due in July 2026. Relevance for CCS: extending free-allocation coverage weakens the implicit carbon price faced by industrial emitters and erodes the marginal incentive to invest in capture retrofits in cement, refining and chemicals — exactly the sectors the EU Net-Zero Industry Act 50 Mtpa-by-2030 storage target depends on. Worth tracking how the eventual Commission proposal handles CCS-eligibility crediting against any free-allocation adjustment.

Chevron–Microsoft "Project Kilby" 2.67 GW Permian gas PPA lands without CCS

Chevron / Bloomberg / CNBC · 22 Jun 2026 · [North America]

Chevron and Microsoft signed a 20-year power purchase agreement to build a 2.67 GW gas-fired generation complex near Pecos (Reeves County, TX) dedicated to a Microsoft data-centre campus, with GE Vernova and Caterpillar Solar Turbines supplying the turbines, first power targeted for 2028 and Chevron FID expected by end-2026. The press materials reference selective catalytic reduction for NOx and brackish-water cooling, but make no mention of carbon capture, CO₂ pipeline tie-in or saline-aquifer storage despite the project sitting on top of one of the best-characterised storage basins in the United States. The deal is the largest behind-the-meter AI-power-deal-without-CCS announced to date, and is a useful real-world data point against the (peer-reviewed) thesis advanced last week by Lau & Tsai in ACS Energy & Fuels that NGCC-plus-CCS could mitigate ~75% of US data-centre emissions by 2030. Worth flagging to anyone modelling future US CCS demand off AI load growth: the willingness-to-pay for CCS on the marquee deals isn't there yet.
