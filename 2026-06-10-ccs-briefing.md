## Projects

- **Spirit Energy / Morecambe Net Zero (MNZ) enters NSTA 'Assess Phase'** — The North Sea Transition Authority confirmed Spirit Energy's CS010 carbon-storage licence has progressed to the Assess Phase, the regulator's penultimate step before a storage permit. The depleted North and South Morecambe fields in the East Irish Sea are characterised for up to ~1 Gt of CO₂ capacity; the MNZ Peak Cluster would transport 3 Mtpa from four Derbyshire/Staffordshire cement and lime plants — covering ~40 % of UK cement and lime emissions. Three years of work including new high-resolution 3D seismic underpin the application. ## Markets & strategy

- **Aalborg Portland signs €2.2 bn (DKK 16.5 bn) ACCSION CCS subsidy contract with Danish Energy Agency** — The Cementir subsidiary, Denmark's single largest CO₂ emitter, formalised the country's third CCS-tender award: a 15-year, inflation-indexed contract-for-difference at €117/t (DKK 875/t) covering up to 1.25 Mtpa of CO₂ capture, transport and onshore storage from 2030. Air Liquide will supply Cryocap™ cryogenic capture (~95 % recovery) on two kiln trains; Harbour Energy will move and store the CO₂. The single project absorbs more than half of Denmark's 2029 CCS-pool target of 2.3 Mtpa and is now the largest committed full-chain onshore CCS value chain in Europe. (Carbon Pulse / gasworld / domain-b / Carbon Herald, 9 Jun 2026) [link](https://www.domain-b.com/environment-1/denmark-awards-ccs-subsidy-aalborg-portland-accsion-project-2026)
