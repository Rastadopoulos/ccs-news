## Projects

- **ExxonMobil surrenders 850,000 acres of Gulf of Mexico federal CO₂-storage leases** — The supermajor is letting go of 163 federal shallow-water leases (~850,000 acres) it acquired in the 2021 and 2023 BOEM sales for under US$25m — acreage prized for sub-seabed CO₂ sequestration — and redirecting capital toward onshore and Texas state-water storage (where it holds the ~271,000-acre Texas GLO lease and permitting is moving); Energy Intelligence ties the retreat to Interior shelving a rule to convert legacy oil-and-gas leases into dedicated CO₂-storage blocks. (Carbon Herald / Energy Intelligence, 3 Jul 2026) [link](https://carbonherald.com/exxonmobil-surrenders-850000-acres-of-gulf-carbon-storage-leases/)

## Still on the radar

- **Ucaneo inaugurates Germany's largest direct air capture plant in Berlin** — A fully electrified electrochemical DAC unit in Berlin-Marzahn rated at 150 t CO₂/yr at >99.9% purity, with part of the CO₂ headed to geological storage (Germany's first verified DAC-plus-storage project); Siemens joins as automation partner and a ~10× commercial follow-on is due to break ground next year. (Carbon Herald / Siemens, 2 Jul 2026) [link](https://carbonherald.com/ucaneo-opens-germanys-largest-dac-plant/)

(Carbon Herald / Iowa Capital Dispatch, 2 Jul 2026) [link](https://carbonherald.com/iowa-regulators-strip-dakotas-from-summit-pipeline-order/)
