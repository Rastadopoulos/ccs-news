Australia opens the world's first commercial carbon refinery on Kooragang Island

Mining Weekly · 18 June 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

MCi Carbon's Myrtle facility was officially opened at Orica's Kooragang Island ammonia plant in Newcastle, NSW, by Federal Climate and Energy Minister Chris Bowen. Built on 15 years of Australian R&D, the demonstration plant uses mineral carbonation to convert up to 2,500 t/yr of CO₂ taken directly from Orica's ammonia stack into roughly 10,000 t/yr of carbonate-embedded materials for concrete, plasterboard, paint, paper, glass and adhesives, with a stated emissions-reduction potential of up to 90% for hard-to-abate industries. Funding totals around A$40 m of Commonwealth and NSW grants plus more than A$40 m of private capital from Orica, ITOCHU Corporation, Mizuho Bank, Sumitomo Mitsui Trust Bank, Mitsubishi UBE Cement Corporation and Austrian refractories major RHI Magnesita, the latter targeting a 50,000 t/yr commercial plant in Austria by 2030.

JERA charters MOL and NYK ammonia carriers to ship CCS-enabled fuel from Louisiana to Japan

Splash247 · 18 June 2026 · [Asia-Pacific / North America]

JERA, Japan's largest power producer, has booked two very large gas carriers from Mitsui OSK Lines (MOL) and another two VLGCs from NYK to transport low-carbon ammonia from the Blue Point project in Ascension Parish, Louisiana, to JERA's Hekinan thermal power station in Aichi, with first shipments scheduled for Japanese FY2029. Blue Point — a US$4 bn joint venture between CF Industries, JERA and Mitsui & Co. — will produce around 1.4 Mtpa of ammonia, capture an estimated 2.3 Mtpa of CO₂ via 1PointFive/Occidental and abate >95% of process emissions, marking the first dedicated low-carbon ammonia trans-Pacific shipping chain anchored on US CCS.

Belgium and UK sign first London Protocol arrangement for cross-border CO₂ storage

Carbon Herald / Fluxys / gov.uk · 16–17 June 2026 · [Europe & UK]

UK Energy Minister Lord Whitehead and Belgian North Sea Minister Annelies Verlinden signed a MoU in Brussels enabling cross-border CO₂ transport from Belgian and other emitters to permanent storage in the UK North Sea, described as the UK's first London Protocol Article 6 bilateral arrangement. The deal sets a regulatory framework for ship- or pipeline-based exports and is intended to unlock the Endurance store and other UK CCS hubs for non-UK emitters.
