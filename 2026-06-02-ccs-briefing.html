Grattan Institute · 1 Jun 2026 · [Australia & APAC] · [NGO]

Influential Melbourne think-tank's Out of Gas: Managing the decline of gas in Australia report finds that any plan to maintain high domestic gas production beyond 2050 implies impracticable deployment of carbon capture and storage, renewable gases and engineered carbon removals. EU Commission's first NZIA progress report — 50 Mtpa CO₂ injection target by 2030

European Commission DG CLIMA · 28 May 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The Commission's first progress report on the Net-Zero Industry Act's 50 Mtpa CO₂ injection target finds Member States have awarded four new storage permits in the past year — versus one between 2009 and 2024. Greensand (Danish North Sea) is expected to start commercial injection "next month", Porthos (Rotterdam) in 2027 and Prinos (Aegean) in 2026–27. A further seven sites adding ~19 Mtpa are in the pipeline. LeadIT Green Cement Tracker — less than 2% of global cement emissions captured by 2035

Energy Live News / LeadIT · 28 May 2026 · [Global]

The updated LeadIT Green Cement Technology Tracker covers 175+ projects worldwide and finds that, even on its central scenario of 38 commercial-scale CCUS projects, only ~58 Mtpa of cement CO₂ will be captured by 2035 — under 2% of global cement-sector emissions. Two flagship projects, Holcim's Obourg plant (Belgium) and Heidelberg Materials' Slite plant (Sweden), have stalled. Heidelberg's Edmonton (Canada) project has slipped from late-2026 to 2028. Brevik (Norway, ~0.4 Mtpa) remains the only operating full-scale cement CCS plant.

University of Queensland unveils CO₂-to-formate electrochemical generator

University of Queensland · 29 May 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

A team at UQ's School of Chemical Engineering — led by Prof. Xiwang Zhang, under the ARC Centre of Excellence for Green Electrochemical Transformation of CO₂ (GETCO₂) — has demonstrated a modular electrochemical generator that converts captured CO₂ and water (with electricity input) into formate, the precursor to formic acid. The cell is designed for on-site deployment at industrial CO₂ point sources, and is being pitched as an import-substitution play: Australia currently imports nearly all of its formic acid from China. The system is pre-commercial, but commercial interest from a formic-acid pipe-antifreeze customer has reportedly been flagged.
