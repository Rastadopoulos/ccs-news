Air Products cancels Ascension Parish CCS facility

Fox8 / WAFB · 30 Jun 2026 · [North America]

Air Products has confirmed it will not proceed with its proposed carbon capture and storage facility in Ascension Parish, Louisiana, ending months of community opposition. Residents near the proposed site had objected to its proximity to homes and schools; the Louisiana Department of Environmental Quality held an air permit hearing in April before the company decided to withdraw. The project would have stored CO₂ underground at one of the largest blue hydrogen complexes contemplated on the US Gulf Coast.

EU NZIA Article 23 storage-obligation deadlines bite from today

Carbon Pulse · 30 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

30 June 2026 is the hard deadline by which EU Member States must have established penalties for breaches of the Net-Zero Industry Act's CO₂ storage obligation, and the first day on which the 44 obligated oil-and-gas producers must begin reporting their progress to the European Commission. Article 23 of the NZIA requires those companies to deliver 50 Mtpa of CO₂ injection capacity collectively by 2030; recent assessments put the trajectory closer to 29 Mtpa, sharpening the spotlight on which jurisdictions will set "effective, proportionate and dissuasive" penalties on schedule.

Singapore and Indonesia close in on Article 6 implementation agreement

Carbon Pulse / local media · 30 Jun 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

Local media report that Singapore and Indonesia are in the final stages of negotiating a bilateral implementation agreement under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement. The deal is one of several Singapore is pursuing across South-East Asia and is closely watched in CCS circles because Indonesia is openly positioning the Asri (Sumatra) and Tangguh (West Papua) hubs to absorb regional CO₂, including from Singapore industrial sources. A finalised text would strengthen the legal scaffolding for cross-border CO₂ flows between Singapore and Indonesia, alongside the existing Singapore–Malaysia and Singapore–Philippines Article 6 instruments.

Spain awards €319m to cement decarbonisation, including CCS at Toral de los Vados

gasworld · 29 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

Spain's Industrial Decarbonisation PERTE programme has allocated €319m (~US$364m) to cement-sector projects, the largest tranche in a €518m round across 17 manufacturers. Cemex receives €200m for its Tarragona plant, including clinker line modernisation; Votorantim Cimentos receives €119m for the Toral de los Vados (León) plant, explicitly including a carbon-capture installation at the El Bierzo cement works. The cement awards are part of a broader €3.17bn PERTE pot designed to mobilise up to €11.8bn of industrial decarbonisation investment.

Deep Sky delivers North America's first certified DAC carbon-removal credits

PR Newswire / BNN Bloomberg · 29 Jun 2026 · [North America]

Deep Sky and Isometric announced that CO₂ permanently injected by Deep Sky Alpha at Innisfail, Alberta, has been independently verified and registered as North America's first certified Direct Air Capture (DAC) carbon-removal credits — and the world's first DAC credits carrying the Core Carbon Principles (CCP) label issued under Isometric's DAC protocol. Microsoft and Royal Bank of Canada will receive the first tranche. Construction-to-injection took ~18 months, on schedule and on budget; offtake partners now include Lufthansa Group, ENGIE, TD Bank and Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation. A meaningful Canadian milestone given the squeeze on industrial CCS economics from Alberta's recent TIER changes (see Markets).
