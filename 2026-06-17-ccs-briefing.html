UP Catalyst unveils Finland graphite production plans using captured CO₂

gasworld · 16 June 2026 · [EU/UK]

Estonia-based UP Catalyst has formalised plans for a 20,000-tonne-per-year battery-grade graphite facility at Oulu Industrial Park in northern Finland, using its Molten Salt Carbon Capture and Electrochemical Transformation (MSCC-ET) process to convert CO₂ captured at Oulun Energia's plants into synthetic graphite. The project — backed by an €18 m EIB venture-debt facility and designation as an EU Strategic Project for critical raw materials — is one of the larger CO₂-utilisation announcements in Europe in 2026 and is positioned to compete with Chinese-dominated graphite supply chains.

Europe needs a 65-ship CO₂ carrier fleet and 33 ports by 2050, Xodus study finds

Marine Link · 16 June 2026 · [EU/UK]

A CCUS Enabling Infrastructure Study released by Xodus for the Net Zero Technology Centre (with EBN, the Port of Rotterdam, Gasunie and Offshore Energies UK) concludes that Europe will need roughly 22 dedicated CO₂ carriers by 2030 scaling to ~65 vessels and 33 enabled ports by 2050, based on 15,000-tonne average ship capacity. The report sees captured CO₂ volumes rising from 70 Mtpa in 2030 to 320 Mtpa by 2050, with shipping carrying around 79 Mtpa even as offshore pipelines scale — and warns that port and vessel investment must start now to meet 2030 deadlines.
