## Projects

- **Deep Sky delivers North America's first certified DAC carbon-removal credits** — Montreal-based Deep Sky, working with London-based certifier Isometric, has delivered the first ICVCM Core Carbon Principles-labelled DAC credits in North America to Microsoft and Royal Bank of Canada, drawn from 14 tonnes injected in May at its Innisfail (Alberta) Deep Sky Alpha facility (designed for ~3,000 t/yr). ## Policy

- **Alberta-Ottawa industrial carbon price reset to $140/t puts Canadian CCS pipeline at risk** — A revised federal-Alberta industrial carbon-price agreement caps the carbon tax at $140/t and flattens the previously legislated escalator that would have reached $170/t by 2030. Varme Energy's shovel-ready $400 million Edmonton waste-to-energy facility with CCS, contracted to process 150,000 t/yr of municipal waste from 2028 and capture >1 Mt of CO₂ over its lifetime, is now at risk of cancellation this autumn; Torchlight's ~$2 billion pulp-mill CCS project at Hinton (1.6 Mt/yr) and "pretty much every" other Canadian CCS project would also be challenged at the lower price. (CBC News, 29 Jun 2026) [https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/calgary/bakx-varme-edmonton-smith-carney-carbon-tax-tier-9.7247705](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/calgary/bakx-varme-edmonton-smith-carney-carbon-tax-tier-9.7247705)

(Carbon Pulse, 29 Jun 2026) [https://carbon-pulse.com/](https://carbon-pulse.com/)
