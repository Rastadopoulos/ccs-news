Orcadian pitches a UK North Sea "Gigagrid" — offshore power with integrated CO₂ capture and reinjection to run a data centre

Orcadian Energy RNS / Upstream · 16 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK]

Orcadian Energy has begun the Assessment Phase for developing the Earlham and Orwell gas fields with a preferred concept of a ~300 MW offshore power station with integrated carbon capture (designed with GE Vernova and Norway's Capsol Technologies), fuelled by the fields' high-CO₂ gas, with the captured CO₂ reinjected into the reservoir and the low-carbon power feeding a co-located offshore AI data centre — the first phase of an "islanded" low-carbon offshore grid. Orcadian has farmed out 50% of the licence P2680 sub-area to the Marine Low Carbon Power Company (MLCP). Because the Earlham gas is too CO₂-rich for economic pipeline export, generate-and-reinject is framed as the optimal monetisation route.

Industry groups urge Baltic-bordering governments to clear the barriers to offshore CO₂ storage

QCIntel · 16 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK] · [paywall]

An alliance of industry stakeholders has called on the governments of Baltic Sea littoral states to address the obstacles still blocking offshore CO₂ storage in the basin, with the Helsinki Convention (HELCOM) singled out as the remaining legal barrier — unlike the London Protocol and OSPAR, which no longer bar sub-seabed storage elsewhere. An independent review of the legality, environmental impact and technical feasibility of CCS in the Baltic has been flagged. A transboundary/regulatory signal for a basin that several EU states are eyeing as shared storage.

Study maps calcium-looping direct air capture as a route to cut cement-manufacturing emissions

ScienceDirect / Joule · 16 Jul 2026 · [Global]

A peer-reviewed analysis in Joule assesses calcium-looping direct air capture for reducing cement-manufacturing emissions, exploiting the synergy between the CaCO₃/CaO sorbent chemistry of DAC and the cement kiln — a pathway toward net-negative cement by cycling sorbent through the DAC unit before calcination. Adds to a growing body of work (a companion Cell Chem Circularity LCA appeared in June) on DAC–cement integration.

Bioforcetech turns sewage sludge into carbon-negative concrete filler — and destroys PFAS in the process

Carbon Herald · 16 Jul 2026 · [North America]

Californian firm Bioforcetech's "OurCarbon" process converts municipal sewage sludge into a CO₂-negative concrete ingredient while breaking down "forever chemicals" (PFAS), diverting organics from methane-emitting landfill. The company won a US$310,000 Bridge Carbontech grant from Columbia University's Carbontech Development Initiative to build a dosing system letting existing concrete batch plants inject the material automatically. A modest but genuine CO₂-utilisation / durable-storage catch.
