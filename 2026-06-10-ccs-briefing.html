Spirit Energy / Morecambe Net Zero enters NSTA 'Assess Phase' on path to UK storage permit

Oilfield Technology / World Oil / Energy Voice · 9 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The North Sea Transition Authority confirmed Spirit Energy's CS010 carbon-storage licence has progressed to the Assess Phase, the regulator's penultimate step before a storage permit. The depleted North and South Morecambe fields in the East Irish Sea are characterised for up to ~1 Gt of CO₂ capacity; the MNZ Peak Cluster would transport 3 Mtpa from four Derbyshire/Staffordshire cement and lime plants — covering ~40 % of UK cement and lime emissions. Three years of subsurface, wells, HSE and engineering work, including new high-resolution 3D seismic acquisition and reprocessing, underpin the application. Project partners estimate ~£1.8 bn GVA to the UK economy by 2050 and ~13,000 supported jobs in construction and operation.

Aalborg Portland signs €2.2 bn ACCSION CCS subsidy contract with Danish Energy Agency

Carbon Pulse / gasworld / domain-b / Carbon Herald · 9 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The Cementir subsidiary — Denmark's single largest CO₂ emitter — formalised the country's third CCS-tender award: a 15-year, inflation-indexed contract-for-difference at €117/t (DKK 875/t) covering up to 1.25 Mtpa of CO₂ capture, transport and onshore storage from 2030, worth up to €147 m/yr (DKK 1.1 bn/yr) and DKK 16.5 bn (~€2.2 bn / US$2.55 bn) over the term. Air Liquide will supply its Cryocap™ cryogenic capture technology on two kiln trains, targeting ~95 % CO₂ recovery; Harbour Energy will deliver transport and permanent onshore storage. The single award absorbs more than half of Denmark's 2029 CCS-pool target of 2.3 Mtpa, locks Aalborg into one of Europe's largest committed full-chain onshore CCS value chains, and provides a high-water-mark CfD reference point for other industrial-CCS tenders.
