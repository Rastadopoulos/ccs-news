Ground Engineering · 30 June 2026 · [APAC — Malaysia]

PETRONAS CCS Solutions Sdn Bhd has awarded Malaysian specialist HELMS Geomarine a long-term contract (initial three years plus a two-year option) for offshore geotechnical services supporting Malaysia's national CCS build-out, including future storage facilities, injection wells, offshore platforms and subsea CO₂ pipelines. Woodside completes operatorship transition of Gippsland Basin JV — adjacent to CarbonNet's Pelican and Kookaburra storage sites

Energy News Bulletin / Woodside · 01 July 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

Effective 01 July 2026, Woodside has assumed operatorship of the Gippsland Basin Joint Venture and the Kipper Unit Joint Venture from ExxonMobil, together with the Longford Gas Plant, Long Island Point liquids plant and associated pipeline infrastructure. Equity remains 50:50 with ExxonMobil in the GBJV (32.5% in KUJV); around 600 ExxonMobil staff transfer to Woodside, and Woodside targets ≥ US$60 million of annual synergies. CCS relevance: the Victorian Government's CarbonNet project is targeting Declaration of Storage Formation for both the Pelican and Kookaburra storage sites in the same Gippsland Basin in 2026, ~10 km offshore from the Bass Strait platforms Woodside now operates — putting a single Australian LNG operator in charge of the pipeline, platform and shore-terminal infrastructure most directly adjacent to Victoria's flagship CCS network.

Louisiana Illuminator: scrapped Air Products project 'could signal stiffer headwinds for CO₂ storage'

Louisiana Illuminator · 02 July 2026 · [North America — US]

Follow-up analysis on Air Products' 30 June cancellation of its $4.5 billion Louisiana Clean Energy Complex frames the withdrawal as more than a one-off commercial decision. Louisiana Governor Jeff Landry's ongoing moratorium on new Class VI carbon-storage well permits is called out as central "regulatory uncertainty"; softening blue-hydrogen demand, higher construction-cost inflation from concurrent industrial and data-centre build-out, and the federal tax bill's cuts to hydrogen fuel credits compound the picture. Air Products will book a pre-tax charge of up to $2.9 billion tied to the cancellation and related exits; industry analysts quoted in the piece warn of ripple effects for other Louisiana CCS-linked petrochemical projects.
