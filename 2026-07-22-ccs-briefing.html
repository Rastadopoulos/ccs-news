Australia's CCUS ambitions undercut by policy gaps, comparative report finds

Carbon Pulse · 21 Jul 2026 · [Australia & APAC] · [paywall]

A new cross-country assessment concludes that Australia's policy environment for carbon capture, utilisation and storage is "among the least enabling" of any high-income nation, despite the country positioning itself as a CCS leader. The report points to gaps in incentives, demand-side support and permitting certainty relative to peers such as the US (45Q), the EU and the UK — a pointed benchmark as Australia's Safeguard Mechanism and offshore acreage programmes mature.

Puro.earth certifies the world's first BECCS-from-biogas project, in Norway

Carbon Herald · 21 Jul 2026 · [Europe & UK]

Inherit Carbon Solutions — with HoopCO₂ and the Northern Lights JV — has become the first validated and verified project to capture biogenic CO₂ from biogas production and store it geologically, earning Europe's first BECCS certification under the Puro Standard. Biogenic CO₂ from the VEAS wastewater plant (serving 800,000+ in the Oslo region) is liquefied, shipped and injected ~2,600 m beneath the North Sea; more than 700 CORCs were issued for the first four months' operation (Feb–May 2026), with ongoing MRV-backed issuances to follow.

Euroports and Normod Carbon sign MoU for a Northern European CO₂ hub

Carbon Herald · 21 Jul 2026 · [Europe & UK]

Belgian-headquartered port operator Euroports and Norwegian CO₂ aggregator Normod Carbon have signed a non-binding memorandum of understanding to develop shared CO₂ hub and shipping infrastructure across Northern Europe, consolidating captured CO₂ from multiple emitters for routing to permanent offshore or onshore storage. It extends Normod's aggregator model — anchored by its planned €250m Port of Grenaa hub (up to 10 Mtpa, first operations 2029) — and is another data point in the maturing liquefied-CO₂ shipping value chain relevant to cross-border storage plays such as Bayu-Undan.
