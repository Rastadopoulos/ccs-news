Moomba CCS Sets Record Australian Carbon Credit Haul After First Year of Operations AU/APAC

Santos · S&P Global Commodity Insights · May 2026

Santos' Moomba CCS project — Australia's largest onshore carbon storage facility — has passed its first full year of operations with 1.3 million tonnes of CO₂e permanently stored to date. The Clean Energy Regulator issued 614,133 Australian Carbon Credit Units (ACCUs) for the project's initial six months (September 2024–March 2025), the single largest issuance ever made under the CCS ACCU method. At full injection rates the project will sequester up to 1.7 Mtpa over its 25-year crediting period at a reported cost of ~A$27–28/tCO₂e, among the lowest globally for large-scale CCS.

Porthos Completes Well Conversions — North Sea First Injection on Track for Autumn 2026 EU

Porthos / Offshore Energy · 5 Feb 2026

The Dutch Porthos partnership confirmed on 5 February 2026 that four former TAQA gas production wells on the P18-A offshore platform have been successfully converted to CO₂ injection wells, while three additional wells have been permanently decommissioned. Underground access points are now ready for injection and secure storage beneath the North Sea seabed. First CO₂ injection remains on course for autumn 2026, with full system operationalisation expected H2 2027; the project will store approximately 2.5 Mtpa for 15 years (37 Mt total), serving Rotterdam-area industrial emitters including Air Liquide, Air Products, ExxonMobil and Shell.

Germany Opens €6bn CCfD Round — CCS and CCU Eligible for the First Time EU

Clean Energy Wire · Jones Day · Fuel Cells Works · Oct 2025 / 2026

Germany's third round of Carbon Contracts for Difference (CCfDs), worth up to €6 billion, has expanded eligibility to include carbon capture and storage (CCS) and carbon capture and utilisation (CCU) projects for the first time, alongside existing support for process decarbonisation in cement, steel, chemicals, pulp and paper, and ceramics. Projects will receive 15-year fixed-price contracts under a reverse-auction mechanism; the preparatory phase launched on 6 October 2025 with participation required by 1 December 2025. The main auction is expected in mid-2026, potentially channelling substantial long-term revenue certainty to hard-to-abate sectors where CCS is the primary decarbonisation pathway.

IRS Issues 45Q Safe Harbor as EPA Subpart RR Faces Elimination North America

US Internal Revenue Service · Baker Botts · Dec 2025

Treasury and the IRS issued Notice 2026-01 in December 2025, providing a safe harbour for taxpayers claiming the Section 45Q carbon capture credit for CO₂ geologically stored during calendar year 2025. The notice responds to the EPA's proposed elimination of Subpart RR monitoring, reporting and verification (MRV) requirements: if the EPA does not relaunch its e-GGRT system by 10 June 2026, taxpayers may substitute an independent engineer or geologist certification of sequestration volumes in lieu of Subpart RR compliance. The guidance applies only to secure geological storage, not CO₂-EOR, and Treasury intends to issue full regulations covering MRV standards.

American Carbon Registry Expands CCS Methodology to Cover DAC, BECCS and BiCRS Global

Carbon Pulse · May 2026

The American Carbon Registry (ACR) has updated its carbon capture and storage methodology to broaden eligibility beyond traditional geological storage of industrial CO₂ to include direct air capture (DAC), bioenergy with carbon capture and storage (BECCS), biomass carbon removal and storage (BiCRS), and certain CO₂-enhanced oil recovery (EOR) projects. The revision allows a wider range of carbon removal approaches to generate tradeable ACR credits under a unified CCS framework, potentially unlocking additional private capital for projects that previously lacked a credible credit pathway.

Svante and Meadow Lake Tribal Council Sign 626,000-Tonne BECCS Offtake with Microsoft North America

BusinessWire · Svante · 6 Apr 2026

North Star Carbon Solutions LP — a partnership between Svante Technologies and the Meadow Lake Tribal Council (MLTC) of Saskatchewan — has signed a 15-year carbon removal offtake agreement with Microsoft for 626,000 tonnes of BECCS-derived CDR credits (up to 90,000 t/yr at capacity). The project will co-locate Svante's solid-sorbent capture technology at the MLTC Bioenergy Centre, capturing CO₂ from waste biomass from an adjacent Indigenous-owned sawmill; captured CO₂ will be transported and stored permanently at a North Star geological storage site. This is Microsoft's first Canadian BECCS CDR offtake, and one of the first such agreements globally to involve full Indigenous ownership of the project entity. Commercial operations are targeted for early 2029.
