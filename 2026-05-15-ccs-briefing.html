EU Commission clears Germany's €6 bn industrial CCS competitive contract scheme

European Commission / Jones Day / ESG News  ·  7 May 2026 Europe

The European Commission approved Germany's €6 billion industrial decarbonisation programme on 7 May 2026 under EU state aid rules, ruling it "necessary and appropriate" for ETS-covered sectors. For the first time, CCS and CCU projects are eligible for Germany's 15-year reverse-auction "climate contracts," with the competitive auction expected mid-2026 covering steel, chemicals, cement, lime, glass, paper, and ceramics. Applications must be submitted by 7 September 2026; only companies that completed the preparatory procedure in late 2025 can bid.

45Q tax credit survives US "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" — FEOC restrictions now live

Carbon Economist / CTVC  ·  Ongoing (enacted Jul 2025, FEOC from Jan 2026) North America

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (enacted 4 July 2025) preserved Section 45Q with base credit values of $50/t for geological storage and $36/t for DAC; wage and apprenticeship multipliers allow effective DAC rates above $130/t. From January 2026, projects with material ties to Foreign Entities of Concern (China, Iran, North Korea, Russia) lose 45Q eligibility — a provision affecting supply-chain sourcing and ownership structures across the US CCUS project pipeline. The preservation of 45Q is broadly regarded as the most important US policy outcome for CCUS investment certainty this cycle.
