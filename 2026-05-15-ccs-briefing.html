Gorgon CCS Posts Worst-Ever Performance in FY2024–25 at 25% Utilisation, AU$265/t Cost

IEEFA · 2026 · [Australia / APAC]

Chevron's Gorgon CCS in Western Australia — the world's largest CCS project — stored only approximately 1.33 Mt of CO₂ in FY2024–25, equivalent to roughly 25% of its regulatory target of ~3.5–4 Mtpa, at a cost of AU$265/t (US$176/t). IEEFA attributes the continuing underperformance to reservoir pressure constraints requiring water-extraction works to expand injection capacity. Gorgon has not met its environmental approval target in any year since operations began in 2019, sustaining ongoing regulatory compliance scrutiny.

45Q Credit Landscape Settles Under “One Big Beautiful Bill” — $85/t Industrial, $180/t DAC

Global CCS Institute / Carbon Capture Coalition · 2025–2026 · [North America]

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed 4 July 2025, P.L. 119-21) cemented the 45Q tax credit at $85/t for industrial and power-sector CCS and $180/t for direct air capture, creating for the first time parity between CO₂ utilisation and geological storage. Transferability is retained for the credit's lifetime, with inflation adjustment commencing 2027 (base year 2025). Analysts caution that real credit value continues to erode against capital-cost inflation; Foreign Entity of Concern restrictions bar Chinese, Russian, Iranian, and North Korean entities from claiming the credit.

Porthos Enters Commercial Operation — Europe’s First Full-Scale CO₂ Transport and Storage Hub

Port of Rotterdam · 2026 · [Europe]

Porthos — the Rotterdam CCS hub backed by the Port of Rotterdam Authority, Gasunie, and EBN, with customers including Air Liquide, Air Products, ExxonMobil, and Shell — entered commercial operation in 2026, the first project in Europe to transport CO₂ from port-area industrial emitters to offshore North Sea geological storage at scale.
