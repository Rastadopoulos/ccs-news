Northern Lights awards second LCO₂ carrier to MISC / "K" Line consortium — 12,000 cbm, dual-fuel LNG, Dalian Shipbuilding

Upstream Online / MISC Berhad / LNG Prime · 4 Jun 2026 · [EU/UK · transboundary · Asia]

Northern Lights JV (Equinor / Shell / TotalEnergies) issued a second time-charter award to the MISC Berhad – Kawasaki Kisen Kaisha ("K" Line) consortium for a 12,000-cbm liquefied-CO₂ carrier with dual-fuel LNG propulsion, to be built by Dalian Shipbuilding Offshore Co. and delivered between H2 2028 and H1 2029. US: Trump administration redirects $185 mln of IIJA carbon-capture appropriations to coal-power build-out under $700 mln DPA package

Latitude Media / US News & World Report · 4 Jun 2026 · [NA]

The White House unveiled a US$700 million coal-industry support package built around the Defense Production Act, allocating US$425 mln to 13 existing coal plants, US$75 mln to a California export terminal, and US$185 mln of DOE grant funding — originally appropriated by Congress under the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law for carbon-capture and storage demonstrations — to build two new coal plants in Alaska and West Virginia and restart a third in Maryland, with selected projects explicitly "not requiring immediate CCUS installation". Former DOE officials and Democratic lawmakers say the diversion is likely an unauthorised use of the CCS appropriation; the broader US$525 mln Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations notice released alongside the package targets "rural reliability" coal projects and ramps down the demonstration-era pull-through of post-combustion capture from the coal fleet. This is the first material divergence in 2026 between the White House and the largely intact 45Q tax credit framework that has continued to anchor private CCS FIDs at Stratos, Bayou Bend and Air Products Louisiana.

Technology & R&D

MIT publishes oxygen-tolerant electrochemical CO₂ capture — N-heterocyclic imines deliver >2 CO₂ per electron, 500 mV above oxygen reduction

Nature Energy / MIT News · 4 Jun 2026 · [NA · peer-reviewed]

An MIT-led team published "Oxygen-tolerant electrochemical CO₂ separation using N-heterocyclic imines with super-stoichiometric release per electron" in Nature Energy (DOI 10.1038/s41560-026-02055-0), reporting a phenylene-linked bis-NHI sorbent that binds CO₂ in the neutral state (50–100 kJ mol⁻¹) and releases it on electro-oxidation. The symmetric bipolar cell maintained >500 mV separation from the parasitic oxygen-reduction potential — the long-standing barrier to direct-air-capture EMCC operating in ambient air — delivered a 2 CO₂ / electron swing via benzene-ring charge delocalisation, and sustained >40 stable cycles. The work materially shortens the path from lab demonstration to commercial DAC stack design and feeds directly into the molecular-sorbent comparator set used by Verdox, Avnos and Mission Zero.

Skytree deploys Stratus DAC for Koppert Cress Dutch greenhouses — fossil-free CO₂ enrichment, up to 1 t/day per unit

gasworld / Koppert Cress · 4 Jun 2026 · [EU/UK]

Amsterdam-based Skytree confirmed deployment of its Stratus direct air capture units at Dutch horticulture group Koppert Cress' new greenhouse in Monster, South-Holland — and at existing sites — replacing combustion-derived or industrial-waste CO₂ enrichment with atmospheric capture at up to 1,000 kg CO₂/day per unit. The deployment is funded via Koppert Cress' innovation arm Division Q (which holds equity in Skytree) and is positioned by both companies as the first commercial-scale DAC deal anchored entirely on greenhouse-grade CO₂ supply rather than carbon-removal credits. The unit-economic relevance is in stripping fossil-CO₂ procurement out of the Dutch horticulture sector — a ~6 Mtpa CO₂ demand pool — and demonstrating a non-credit-dependent revenue stack for sub-MW DAC.

Deep Sky signs 10-year DAC offtake with TD Bank Group — 18,000+ verified Canadian removal credits

Carbon Pulse / Globe and Mail / BNN Bloomberg / Deep Sky · 4 Jun 2026 · [NA]

Montréal-headquartered Deep Sky announced a 10-year direct-air-capture carbon-removal agreement with Toronto-Dominion Bank Group covering more than 18,000 verified DAC credits to be sourced from Deep Sky's Innisfail, Alberta multi-technology test centre (operating since August 2025) and the company's planned commercial facility in south-western Manitoba. Financial terms were not disclosed. The TD deal follows Deep Sky's earlier offtakes with Engie and Lufthansa Group, and is the first major Canadian financial-services anchor commitment to engineered, third-party-registry permanent removal — strengthening Canada's bid to ride the post-OBBB 45Q environment without leaning on US-only voluntary demand pools.

MODEC × Eld Energy sign Tokyo JDA for integrated 1.2 MW SOFC + CO₂-capture FPSO power system

World Oil / Offshore Energy / MODEC · 4 Jun 2026 · [Asia · EU/UK · Norway]

FPSO supplier MODEC and Norwegian fuel-cell developer Eld Energy signed a Joint Development Agreement in Tokyo — during HRH Crown Prince Haakon of Norway's state visit and in the presence of Norway's Minister of Trade and Industry — to scale their integrated solid-oxide-fuel-cell + post-combustion CO₂ capture system from the existing 120 kW pilot to a 1.2 MW unit targeted for onshore testing in 2029, ahead of long-term offshore demonstration on a future FPSO. Eld will own design, procurement and construction of the SOFC block; MODEC will own the capture island and topside integration. The agreement is the most concrete OEM step yet toward a near-zero-emissions FPSO power train for Brazilian pre-salt and Australian offshore basins, and complements MODEC's existing CCUS partnerships with Petrobras and Mitsubishi.
