State of Carbon Dioxide Removal — 3rd Edition published

State of CDR / Carbon Brief · 3 Jun 2026 · [Global]

The annual Oxford-led global CDR assessment finds humanity currently removes ~2.2 GtCO2/yr (≈5% of gross emissions), almost entirely through conventional land-sink methods. To stay within a 1.5 °C pathway, CDR must scale roughly fourfold to 8.75 GtCO2/yr by 2050 — requiring deployment rates comparable to, or faster than, solar PV and electric vehicles. National pledges currently fall short of that pathway by more than 5 GtCO2/yr by 2050. Novel CDR (DAC, BECCS, mineralisation, ocean alkalinity enhancement) accounts for less than 0.1% of today's total. The authors flag 2026–30 as the "crucial window" for establishing CDR's policy and finance architecture.
