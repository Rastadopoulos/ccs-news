Policy

EU tables sweeping ETS overhaul for 2031–40, with a first-ever channel to fund permanent CO₂ removals

Reuters · 17 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK]

The European Commission proposed its Phase 5 revision of the EU Emissions Trading System, aligning it with the 90%-by-2040 net-emissions target while easing pressure on industry: the annual cap-decline rate is cut to ~3.7% (from 4.3%) from 2031, free allocation is extended to end-2037, and 80% of free permits are made conditional on firms investing in EU decarbonisation. For carbon management the proposal expands Modernisation Fund eligibility to explicitly cover CCS/CCU, and raises the cap by 250 million allowances to be auctioned across 2031–2040 with the revenue used to centrally purchase certified permanent carbon-removal units — the first mechanism to pull engineered removals (incl. BECCS/DAC with permanent storage) into the ETS, though analysts question whether enough high-quality removals can be delivered in time.

Peer-reviewed schema tackles regional storage characterisation for Class VI wells in Kansas's Arbuckle Group

Nature / Scientific Reports · 18 Jul 2026 · [North America]

A paper in Scientific Reports proposes a schema for basin/regional-scale geological evaluation to support US EPA Class VI well applications in the Arbuckle Group — a thick, stacked Cambrian–Ordovician carbonate saline reservoir underlying Kansas and Oklahoma, in a seismically sensitive region. It targets the storage-capacity, reservoir-pressure and caprock-integrity uncertainties that earlier plume-only Arbuckle modelling left open — a transferable methodology as more jurisdictions build out permitting-grade site assessment.

Green groups say the ETS overhaul "caves to industrial laggards"; CCS industry wants removals cash ring-fenced

European Environmental Bureau / CCS Europe · 17 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK] · [NGO]

Reaction to the Commission's ETS revision split sharply. The European Environmental Bureau warned the proposal "caves to industrial laggards", arguing the softer cap trajectory, extended free permits and new flexibilities reward polluters and weaken incentives to decarbonise. CCS Europe, by contrast, welcomed the integration of permanent removals as a chance to "kickstart" a market for stored industrial CO₂ but urged the Commission to ring-fence dedicated CCS funding rather than leave it to compete inside a broadened Modernisation Fund.
