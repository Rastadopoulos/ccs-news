European Commission tables EU ETS Phase 5 reform: 90% cut by 2040 and a ~€50bn carbon-removal purchasing mechanism

ICAP · 17 July 2026 (Carbon Herald deep-dive 20 July) · [Europe & UK]

The 17 Jul proposal aligns the ETS with a binding 90% net-reduction target for 2040, makes free allocation conditional on verified decarbonisation investment plans from 2031, and — most consequential for CCS/CDR — adds ~250 million allowances whose auction revenue a central Removals Authority would use to procure certified BioCCS and DACCS units, ramping to ~48 Mt/yr by 2040 (an estimated €50bn compliance market; biochar excluded initially; up to 260m international credits permitted 2036–40). NGO reaction split on day one — Bellona argues the softer trajectory and extended free-allocation phase-out weaken the investment signal. Now to Parliament/Council.

UK government responds to the independent GGR review: revenue-certainty business model, non-pipeline transport pathfinder, and a UK GGR Standard

GOV.UK / DESNZ · 17 July 2026 · [Europe & UK]

DESNZ committed to kickstarting engineered removals via the GGR Business Model (top-up revenue-certainty payments for BECCS and DACCS), a Non-Pipeline Transport Pathfinder to open CCS beyond the existing clusters, a UK GGR Standard aligned with the EU CRCF with mandatory MRV, "net-zero-ready" requirements for new energy-from-waste plants, and integration of GGRs into the UK ETS "as soon as practicable".
