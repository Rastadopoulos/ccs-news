## Projects

- **Alberta, Ottawa and oil-sands producers sign MOU to advance the Pathways CCS project — with the capture target cut to ~16 Mtpa** — The Alberta and federal governments and the five Oil Sands Alliance members (Canadian Natural, Cenovus, ConocoPhillips Canada, Imperial, Suncor) signed a memorandum of understanding to advance the multibillion-dollar Pathways CO₂ transport-and-storage project — a pipeline network carrying captured CO₂ from northern Alberta oil-sands sites to a storage hub near Cold Lake — with Ottawa committing to extend carbon-capture investment tax credits to 2035 and Alberta to finalise its own incentive program. The agreement targets ~16 Mtpa of capture by 2035, down ~77% from the alliance's original 68 Mt/yr ambition (and below the earlier 22 Mtpa-by-2030 pledge), and is tied to support for a proposed ~1 million bbl/d Alberta–BC oil pipeline — drawing criticism that the climate goal has been materially diluted. (Reuters / BNN Bloomberg / Canada's National Observer, 13 Jul 2026) [link](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/canadas-oil-sands-alliance-reaches-agreement-advance-pathways-carbon-capture-2026-07-13/)
- **JAPEX establishes Tomakomai CCS Co. to run Japan's first "specified-area" offshore storage project** — Japan Petroleum Exploration (JAPEX) announced it has established a wholly-owned subsidiary, Tomakomai CCS Co., Ltd. (TCCS), on 1 July to serve as the implementing entity for the CCS project in the Tomakomai area off Hokkaido. JAPEX will apply for government approval to transfer its offshore exploratory drilling permit to TCCS, which will then apply to METI for a CO₂ storage business permit; the project targets CO₂ from Idemitsu's Hokkaido Refinery and HEPCO's Tomato-Atsuma power station, within the offshore acreage designated in February 2025 as Japan's first "specified area" under the CCS Business Act. (JAPEX, 13 Jul 2026) [link](https://www.japex.co.jp/en/news/detail/20260713_01/)
