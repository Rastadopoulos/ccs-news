Vallourec and Syngular Solutions sign MoU to develop BECCS projects in Brazil

GlobeNewswire · 22 May 2026 · [South America]

French premium tubular manufacturer Vallourec signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Syngular Solutions — Brazil’s leading BECCS and CCUS engineering and consulting firm — to jointly develop bioenergy with carbon capture and geological storage (BECCS) projects in Brazil. The partnership targets the country’s large ethanol industry as a source of biogenic CO², which would be captured and permanently stored in depleted oil and gas reservoirs or deep saline aquifers. Vallourec contributes its tubular and well-completion expertise for CO² injection; Syngular brings project development, regulatory navigation and customer relationships with ethanol producers. No specific project capacities or timelines were disclosed in the MoU announcement.

Santos Moomba CCS stores 2 million tonnes CO²e — milestone reached in 18 months

Santos (ASX announcement) · ~19 May 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

Santos announced that its Moomba CCS project in South Australia’s Cooper Basin has safely and permanently stored 2 million tonnes of CO² equivalent since first injection in September 2024 — achieved in just over 18 months despite two significant flooding events in the basin. The project stored approximately 1 Mt CO²e in the 2024/25 reporting period alone, equivalent to roughly one-third of total reductions in Safeguard Mechanism covered emissions across Australia for that year. Santos flagged the acreage’s potential to support injection of up to 20 Mtpa for up to 50 years, positioning Moomba as the foundation of a future commercial third-party storage business serving hard-to-abate sectors such as steel and cement.

Bigadan inaugurates EU’s first commercial biogenic CO² capture-to-storage chain at Kalundborg

Bioenergy Insight / Gasworld · 20 May 2026 · [Europe — Denmark]

Danish biogas company Bigadan formally inaugurated its CO² facility at Kalundborg Bioenergi, establishing what it describes as one of the world’s first fully integrated commercial value chains for biogenic CO² capture, liquefaction, marine transport and permanent subsea storage. The facility initially captures 27,000 tonnes of CO²/year from biogas fed by agricultural and industrial residues; the gas is routed offshore for storage through Project Greensand in the Danish North Sea. Bigadan also operates a capture site at Horsens and is developing a third in Herning, targeting 200,000 tonnes/year across its Danish biogas portfolio by 2030. CCO Henrik Iversen called it “the beginning of a new industry” for biogenic CCS in Europe.

Canada-Alberta oilsands deal quietly cuts Pathways CCS capture target from 22 to 16 Mtpa

Canada’s National Observer · 20 May 2026 · [North America — Canada]

Implementation documents from the Canada-Alberta energy agreement signed by PM Carney and Premier Smith reveal the Oil Sands Alliance (formerly Pathways Alliance) carbon capture target was reduced from 22 Mtpa by 2030 to 16 Mtpa by 2035 — a concession not mentioned in the public announcement. The revised carbon price floor under the deal is $130/tonne, well below the $170/tonne set under the previous Liberal government. Project costs have already risen from the original $16.5 billion estimate to more than $20 billion. Critics argue the scaled-back targets are insufficient given planned oilsands production expansion and undercut the rationale of tying a new Pacific pipeline to CCS delivery.
