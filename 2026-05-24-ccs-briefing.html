Pathways Alliance oilsands CCS megaproject quietly lowered its CO₂ capture targets in Ottawa-Alberta deal

Canada's National Observer  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [North America]

The flagship $20 billion-plus Pathways/Oil Sands Alliance CCS project — linking 20 oilfield capture facilities to a Cold Lake injection hub — had its CO₂ capture commitments scaled back as part of the Canada-Alberta energy MOU, with the agreed industrial carbon price set at C$130/t by 2040, well below the previous Liberal government's C$170/t target. Global CCS Institute profiles Brevik CCS — world's largest cement CO₂ capture facility

Global CCS Institute  ·  19 May 2026  ·  [Europe / Norway]

The GCCSSI released a new facility profile on Heidelberg Materials' Brevik CCS project in Norway, which captures approximately 400,000 t/yr of CO₂ from cement production — the largest cement capture plant in the world. Liquefied CO₂ is shipped to the Northern Lights terminal and injected 2,600 m beneath the North Sea seabed. The facility has been in commercial operation since its June 2025 inauguration, producing "evoZero" carbon-captured cement. Northern Lights Phase 1 (1.5 Mt/yr) is fully booked; Phase 2 FID was taken in March 2026 to expand to ≥5 Mt/yr by ~2030.

Abu Dhabi issues its first-ever general carbon capture policy — GCCSSI commentary

Global CCS Institute  ·  18 May 2026  ·  [MENA]

The Emirate of Abu Dhabi published a General Policy on Carbon Capture, the first instrument of its kind in the UAE and a significant step toward formalising CCUS within Emirati industrial decarbonisation strategy. The Global CCS Institute released commentary noting the policy represents "an important milestone … in the evolution of carbon policy" and could provide a regulatory foundation for CCS project development in one of the world's largest hydrocarbon-producing regions. ADNOC operates the Al Reyadah CCS facility (800,000 t/yr from direct reduced iron).
