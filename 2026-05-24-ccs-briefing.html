Alberta Expects Carbon Capture Deal With Oil Sands Alliance Within Two Months

Bloomberg  ·  22 May 2026  ·  [North America]

Alberta Premier Danielle Smith said she expects a trilateral funding agreement between the province, Ottawa and the Oil Sands Alliance (Canadian Natural, Cenovus, ConocoPhillips, Imperial, Suncor) to be finalised within “one to two months.” The deal would unlock the world’s largest planned CCS network: 20 oilfield capture plants linked by pipeline to a central injection hub near Cold Lake, AB. Revised targets under the Canada–Alberta Implementation Agreement set capture at 6 Mt CO₂/yr by 2035 and 16 Mt/yr by 2045 — a marked reduction from the original pledge of 22 Mt/yr by 2030. The CCS MOU is also a stated precondition for approval of a new oil pipeline to the BC coast.

Santos Moomba CCS Passes 2 Million Tonnes CO₂e in Just 18 Months

Santos  ·  21 May 2026  ·  [Australia & APAC — Projects]

Santos’ Moomba CCS project (Cooper Basin, South Australia) has permanently stored 2 Mt CO₂e — reached in just over 18 months despite two major flooding events. The project stored roughly 1 Mt CO₂e in the 2024/25 reporting period, accounting for approximately one-third of covered-sector emissions reductions under Australia’s Safeguard Mechanism for the year. Lifecycle cost is below US$30/t. Santos says Cooper/Eromanga Basin geology could support up to 20 Mt/yr for 50 years, and is positioning Moomba CCS as the foundation of a third-party commercial storage business targeting hard-to-abate sectors such as steel and cement.

Pathways / Oil Sands Alliance Cuts CCS Targets Sharply in Ottawa–Alberta Deal

Canada’s National Observer  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [North America — Policy]

The flagship C$20B+ Pathways CCS megaproject formally reduced its CO₂ capture goal from 22 Mt/yr by 2030 to 6 Mt/yr by 2035 and 16 Mt/yr by 2045 as part of the Ottawa–Alberta oil sands “grand bargain” signed 15 May. Critics note that Ottawa is covering up to 50% of capital costs while the revised targets significantly reduce the project’s claimed climate impact. A final trilateral MOU between the federal government, Alberta and the Oil Sands Alliance remains outstanding — the step Premier Smith is now publicly targeting for within two months (see Bloomberg item, 22 May, above).

Durable CDR Contracts Reach 2.3 Mt in Q1 2026 — Largest Opening Quarter on Record

Carbon Herald / CDR.fyi  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [Markets & Corporate — Global]

The CDR.fyi tracker recorded 2.3 Mt of durable carbon removal contracted in Q1 2026, equal to 560% of Q1 2025 volume. Microsoft alone committed 1 Mt; 113+ other buyers contracted 1.3 Mt — the third-highest non-Microsoft quarterly volume on record. Biochar dominated at 93% of volumes contracted. High-durability geological removal (DACS/geological CCS-backed CDR) remains a small fraction of total volumes. The report also notes Canada’s launch of the world’s first federal CDR procurement programme (C$10M, March 2026) and the Advance Carbon Removal Coalition targeting C$100M by 2030.
