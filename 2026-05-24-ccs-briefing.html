Climeworks CEO: Carbon Removal Market Can’t Be Built on Microsoft Alone

Bloomberg Green  ·  22 May 2026  ·  [Global / DAC]

Microsoft has put new carbon-removal purchases on hold, rattling a market in which the tech giant accounts for roughly 80% of all contracted durable CDR tonnes. Climeworks co-CEO Christoph Gebald told Bloomberg the pause should spur market diversification rather than collapse, arguing the sector must deepen its buyer base beyond a single anchor purchaser. Microsoft did not deny a slowdown but declined to confirm when it might resume. The episode highlights structural fragility in the nascent high-quality CDR market, with implications for DAC developers including Heirloom, 1PointFive and Carbfix that depend on large offtake agreements to justify capital deployment.

Santos Moomba CCS Reaches 2 Million Tonnes CO₂e Stored in 18 Months

Santos (press release)  ·  21 May 2026  ·  [Australia / APAC — Projects]

Santos announced its Moomba CCS project in South Australia’s Cooper Basin has permanently stored 2 million tonnes of CO₂e in just over 18 months of operation, despite two significant flooding events. The project stores approximately 1.7 Mt CO₂/yr, equalling roughly one-third of the total reduction in Safeguard Mechanism covered emissions in 2024/25. Santos has received more than 1.19 million ACCUs for verified injections through September 2025 and is exploring third-party storage access, with the basin’s geology estimated to support up to 20 Mt/yr for five decades.

Lufthansa Group Signs Multi-Year CDR Offtake Spanning DAC, Biochar and Regenerative Agriculture

Senken / PR Newswire  ·  21 May 2026  ·  [Europe — Markets]

Senken announced a multi-year carbon-removal offtake with Europe’s largest airline group covering three pathways: DAC credits from Deep Sky’s Alpha facility in Canada (3,000 t/yr nameplate, online August 2025); industrial biochar from Exomad Green in Bolivia; and regenerative agriculture via Klim in Germany. Permanent carbon removal now accounts for roughly 20% of Lufthansa’s climate portfolio, double the prior year. A separate Deep Sky–Lufthansa Group DAC credit agreement was disclosed the same day.

Mantel and Wood Partner to Scale Molten-Borate High-Temperature Carbon Capture for Oil Sands

GlobeNewswire  ·  19 May 2026  ·  [North America — Technology]

Mantel Capture designated Wood as its preferred fired-equipment partner across commercial deployments. Their first joint project is a FEED for a SAGD facility in Western Canada designed to capture approximately 60,000 t CO₂/yr from steam generators and boilers using Mantel’s molten-borate capture technology. The partnership addresses a long-standing gap: conventional amine solvents struggle in high-temperature, oxygen-rich flue streams common in oil-sands operations.
