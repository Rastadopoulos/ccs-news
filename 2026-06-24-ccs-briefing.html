UK Environment Agency consultation on Drax BECCS permit variation closes today

gov.uk · Environment Agency · 24 Jun 2026 · [Europe/UK]

The Environment Agency's public consultation on its 'minded-to-grant' draft decision for the permit variation at Drax Power Station in Yorkshire closes 24 June. The agency has indicated it can find no reason to refuse the variation, which would allow Drax to install post-combustion capture on its biomass units and pipe the captured CO₂ to North Sea storage as part of the proposed BECCS scheme. A final determination follows once consultation responses are reviewed. The decision is politically charged: Drax has earlier this year signalled it is paring back BECCS investment pending policy clarity, and the project remains under a separate legal challenge to the 2024 development consent order.

EU Horizon Europe TITAN project validates biogas-to-hydrogen pathway with solid-carbon co-product

EBA News · 23 Jun 2026 · [Europe] · via Shelly Murrell weekly monitoring

The 48-month Horizon Europe TITAN project is nearing completion, having demonstrated a process that converts raw biogas into hydrogen and solid carbon. The solid-carbon co-product is presented as a permanent, geologically stable storage form for the carbon content of the biogas — a hybrid CCS / CDR pathway distinct from conventional capture-plus-injection routes. Industrial relevance for off-grid biogas streams (agricultural, wastewater) where pipeline-grade biomethane upgrading or sub-surface CO₂ injection is uneconomic.

Gevo formalises BECCS-anchored carbon-removal business with new credits platform

GlobeNewswire (via Manila Times) · 23 Jun 2026 · [North America]

Gevo announced an expanded focus on the ~US$12 bn carbon-removal market and launched gevocarbon.com to consolidate sales of its carbon abatement and Puro.earth-certified carbon-removal credits across compliance and voluntary markets. The company anchors the offering on its operational BECCS facility at the Gevo North Dakota ethanol plant, which has now issued more than 500,000 high-quality CDR credits — by Gevo's own count, the largest cumulative issuance of any engineered-removal supplier. First-half 2026 sales already exceed full-year 2025 totals. Timing coincides with London Climate Action Week and signals Gevo positioning carbon — rather than ethanol — as the primary growth engine.
