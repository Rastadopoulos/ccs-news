Aircapture wins Tencent CarbonX 2.0 award for microwave-driven DAC; 100 t/yr Kenya pilot mandated

PRNewswire / Carbon Herald · 25 Jun 2026 · [North America / Africa]

Berkeley-based Aircapture has been named one of 16 winners in Tencent's CarbonX 2.0 program for its Lightswing™ microwave direct-air-capture process, which uses targeted microwave energy to break the CO₂-sorbent bond and avoids the bulk-thermal regeneration penalty that dominates conventional DAC energy budgets. Award conditions include scaling Lightswing to a 100-bed system capturing 100 tonnes CO₂/year in Kenya's Rift Valley, where basalt geology enables in-situ mineralisation; Aircapture will share a Kenyan hub with three other DAC technology vendors. Relevant to the Otway CRC-3 mineralisation work programme as a comparator on microwave-regenerated solid sorbents.
