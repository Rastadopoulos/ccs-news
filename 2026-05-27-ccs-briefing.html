California Achieves First CO&sub2; Injection at Carbon TerraVault I — State’s Inaugural Commercial CCS Project

California Resources Corporation (via GlobeNewswire)  ·  26 May 2026  ·  [North America — USA]

California Resources Corporation (CRC) completed the first commercial CO&sub2; injection at Carbon TerraVault I (CTV I) at its Elk Hills Field in Kern County, marking California’s first operational carbon capture and storage project. The site holds the state’s first final US EPA Class VI underground injection permits. CTV I has annual storage capacity of up to 1.46 million metric tonnes of CO&sub2;, with total reservoir capacity of 38 million metric tonnes; the CO&sub2; is sourced from CRC’s cryogenic gas plant at Elk Hills and injected more than one mile underground into a depleted oil and gas reservoir. CRC has submitted eight additional CTV reservoirs for permitting, representing approximately 352 million metric tonnes of further storage potential, and committed more than USD 1 million to a Community Benefits Plan for Kern County. A Community Advisory Council will be formed in 2026.

Industry Groups Call for Phased EU Approach to CO&sub2; Transport Regulation

Carbon Herald  ·  23 May 2026  ·  [Europe] Policy  ·  via Shelly Murrell weekly monitoring

A coalition of 21 industry stakeholders published a joint statement urging the European Commission to adopt a flexible, phased regulatory framework for CO&sub2; transport infrastructure, warning that overly prescriptive rules introduced too early could undermine investor confidence and delay first-mover projects. Key recommendations include streamlined permitting, improved cross-border coordination, support for regional CO&sub2; transport corridors (pipeline and non-pipeline), and targeted de-risking mechanisms. The statement aims to shape the Commission’s upcoming CO&sub2; Market and Infrastructure Framework legislation, expected in Q3 2026.

COFs Redefine Membrane Limits in Carbon Capture

Mirage News  ·  25 May 2026  ·  [Global] Technology  ·  via Shelly Murrell weekly monitoring

Researchers at Tohoku University report that heteroatom-engineered covalent organic framework (COF) mixed matrix membranes achieve CO&sub2;/CH&sub4; and CO&sub2;/H&sub2; separation performance surpassing the Robeson upper bound — a long-standing benchmark for gas separation membranes. The COF pore chemistry simultaneously enhances selective CO&sub2; adsorption and enables rapid molecular transport, directly addressing the traditional permeability–selectivity trade-off that has constrained membrane-based capture systems. The underlying research appeared in the Journal of the American Chemical Society (21 May 2026) and is relevant to post-combustion, natural gas processing, and pre-combustion hydrogen production applications.
