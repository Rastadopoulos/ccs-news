Federal Environment Minister makes administrative determination on Browse CCS — project to be assessed wholly under EPBC Act

Carbon Pulse / Mirage News · 24 Jun 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

Australia's Federal Minister for the Environment and Water has issued an administrative determination that Woodside Energy's proposed Browse offshore carbon capture and storage component will be assessed wholly under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999, following Woodside's withdrawal of the earlier referral and resubmission under the post-Tranche 1 EPBC framework that took effect in February. The associated upstream gas development is being opened separately to public comment. Browse is a joint venture between Woodside (operator), BP, Mitsui & Co., Mitsubishi Corporation and PetroChina International, and the CCS scheme proposes aggregating CO₂ via pipeline for injection into the depleted Angel reservoir, with a stated potential of up to ~4 Mtpa. The federal-only assessment pathway removes one layer of state/federal duplication but does not pre-empt the Scott Reef impact questions that drove the original referral's withdrawal.

State of Carbon Dioxide Removal — 3rd Edition published at London Climate Action Week

stateofcdr.org (LSE-Grantham et al.) · 24 Jun 2026 · [Global]

The third edition of the independent scientific State of CDR assessment was released at the LSE-Grantham / AlliedOffsets "Building the CDR Market of Tomorrow" event during London Climate Action Week. Headline findings: cumulative committed capital across the carbon dioxide removal sector has reached ~US$11.5 bn as of Q1 2026 and current global CDR capacity stands at ~18.1 Mt CO₂e/yr; novel CDR (DAC, BECCS, enhanced weathering, biochar, ocean methods) is growing at ~40% annually, but to meet 1.5 °C pathways global CDR would need to scale roughly four-fold to ~8.75 Gt CO₂/yr by 2050. The report flags 2026–2030 as the decisive window for "establishing CDR's role" — with current national pledges leaving a ~5 Gt CO₂/yr removal gap by mid-century. Relevant context for CCS/BECCS roadmaps and for CO2CRC's monitoring/MRV positioning given the report's emphasis on durability and verification standards.

'Permanence Trust' mechanism for carbon-removal reversal risk unveiled at London Climate Action Week

Carbon Pulse · 24 Jun 2026 · [Global]

Experts speaking at London Climate Action Week revealed a proposed market-wide "Permanence Trust" mechanism to mitigate reversal risk in carbon-removal credits beyond the standard project life horizon. A pilot is scheduled to launch later this year. The design responds to a structural weakness in the voluntary CDR market: removals priced today (afforestation, BECCS, enhanced weathering, mineralisation, geological CO₂ storage with non-perpetual liability transfer) carry a tail risk of CO₂ release decades into the future that no individual project can fully underwrite. The Trust is being positioned as a collective backstop. Particularly relevant to CO₂CRC's discussion of long-term storage liability frameworks under the Offshore Petroleum and Greenhouse Gas Storage Act and analogous regimes.
