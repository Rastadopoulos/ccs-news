INPEX spuds Japan's first offshore CO₂-storage appraisal well off Chiba

Upstream Online / Rigzone · 10 Jul 2026 · [Aus/APAC — Japan] · [paywall]

INPEX (85%) and Kanto Natural Gas Development (15%), through their Metropolitan CCS JV, have begun drilling the Kujukuri-Oki J-1 appraisal well ~5 km off Kujukuri, Chiba — the first offshore CO₂-storage appraisal well drilled in Japan. The METI/JOGMEC-backed Advanced CCS project (drilling approved 15 April) targets CO₂ from Nippon Steel's Kimitsu works and other Keiyo industrial-zone emitters, with an initial 1.3 Mtpa injection rate scalable to 5 Mtpa, commercial storage in the early 2030s and FID targeted for 2027; a second well (J-2) follows.

Fugro wins Saipem survey contract for UK's first offshore CO₂ pipeline (Northern Endurance)

Offshore Energy / OEDigital · 10 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK — United Kingdom]

Fugro has been contracted by Saipem to provide integrated marine survey and inspection services for the Northern Endurance Partnership (NEP) — the bp/Equinor/TotalEnergies JV building the UK's first offshore CO₂ transport-and-storage system. The scope covers nearshore and offshore surveys, installation monitoring and third-party crossings along the 145-km offshore pipeline to subsea injection facilities in the Endurance saline aquifer (~1,000 m below the North Sea, up to ~1 Gt storage capacity). Storage is targeted to start in 2028.

Gasunie-commissioned PwC study finds CCS the cheapest route to decarbonise Dutch industry

Gasunie / ChemXplore · 10 Jul 2026 · [Europe/UK — Netherlands]

A PwC study for Dutch gas-infrastructure operator Gasunie concludes the most cost-effective decarbonisation of Dutch industry runs through CCS, low-carbon hydrogen and hybrid electrification. CCS is modelled to abate ~21 Mt CO₂/yr (of which ~18 Mt via blue-hydrogen production), with steel ~9 Mt and hybrid boilers ~6 Mt — but the study warns that high energy costs, grid congestion and infrastructure timing are pushing companies to postpone investment.

IEEFA warns offshore CCS ambitions vastly outpace capability

IEEFA / GreentechLead · 10 Jul 2026 · [Global — offshore] · [NGO]

A new IEEFA report (analyst Anika Juhn) argues offshore CO₂-storage ambitions are running far ahead of operational reality: scaling from ~3.6 Mt stored today toward targets near 140 Mtpa implies a ~40-fold expansion while requiring secure containment for centuries, and today's operating experience is insufficient to underwrite such aggressive plans. It cites Northern Lights' ~US$714m phase-2 to reach 5 Mtpa by 2028. A financial-risk critique that feeds the CCS-sceptic narrative.
