Oil Sands Alliance cuts Pathways CCS target — 22 Mtpa goal by 2030 revised down to 16 Mtpa by 2035

Canada’s National Observer · 20 May 2026 · [North America]

An implementation agreement struck between Ottawa and Alberta’s oil-sands industry includes a revised carbon-capture commitment for the Pathways CCS network. The Oil Sands Alliance (Canadian Natural, Cenovus Energy, ConocoPhillips, Imperial, Suncor) has backpedalled from its original pledge to capture 22 million tonnes per annum of COℶ by 2030, replacing it with a lower target of 16 Mtpa by 2035. The Pathways network is designed as a COℶ pipeline-and-hub system linking oil-field capture plants north of Edmonton to an underground injection site. The revision follows sustained pressure on capex as oil prices moderated and federal-provincial negotiations over carbon pricing and industrial subsidies extended into 2026.

Carbon removal marks largest quarter on record — 2.3 Mt contracted in Q1 2026

Carbon Herald / CDR.fyi · 20 May 2026 · [Global / Markets]

The CDR.fyi platform’s Q1 2026 report found that durable carbon removal contracted volumes reached 2.3 million tonnes — roughly 560% of the same quarter in 2025 and the largest single quarter on record. Microsoft anchored the period with a 1 million tonne agreement; more than 113 other purchasers contracted the remaining 1.3 Mt. Technologies represented span geological CCS (including BECCS and DACCS), biochar, mineralisation and enhanced rock weathering. The data suggests the CDR market is crossing an inflexion point from niche purchases toward multi-buyer, multi-pathway procurement programmes.

APAC buyers’ coalition launched in Singapore to accelerate regional climate finance

Carbon Herald · 20 May 2026 · [Asia & Pacific]

A new buyers’ coalition was launched in Singapore on 20 May to pool demand-side commitments for carbon removal and low-carbon offtake across Asia. The initiative aims to address the chicken-and-egg problem facing CCS and CDR developers in the region: project developers need offtake certainty before committing capital, while buyers await credible project pipelines. The coalition is backed by a group of Asian corporates and is expected to publish procurement criteria and a joint request-for-proposals process in H2 2026. The development is relevant to Australian offshore storage aspirations, given ongoing dialogue about Japan, South Korea and Singapore importing COℶ for permanent storage in Australian basins.
