Korean CCUS delegation tours Pilot Energy’s Cliff Head offshore CCS site, Western Australia

Carbon Herald  ·  23 May 2026  ·  [Australia & APAC]

Eleven Korean industry and government organisations — including Korea National Oil Corporation, Samsung, SK, Hyundai, and the Korea Export-Import Bank — visited Pilot Energy’s Cliff Head Carbon Capture and Storage (CHCCS) project off the Western Australian coast as part of the Australia–Korea CCUS Industry Seminar held in Perth. The CHCCS project, the first stage of Pilot Energy’s Mid West Clean Energy Project, aims to convert the depleted Cliff Head offshore oilfield into a permanent CO₂ storage facility with over 1 Mtpa of capacity. The visit underscores active Korean interest in securing offshore storage capacity for industrial decarbonisation at home, organised with support from Austrade.

Japan sets permitting standards for seabed drilling in conservation areas for offshore CCS

Carbon Pulse  ·  22 May 2026  ·  [Asia-Pacific]  ·  [paywall]

Japan amended its environmental conservation law to establish explicit permitting standards for seabed drilling in designated conservation zones, specifically to enable offshore CCS exploration and development. The update is a meaningful regulatory advance for Japan’s national CCS programme, which includes multiple JOGMEC-supported projects — among them the Metropolitan CCS joint venture (INPEX / Kanto Natural Gas Development) targeting storage beneath the seabed off the Kujukuri coast in Chiba Prefecture, with CO₂ captured from Tokyo’s Keiyo industrial complex. Storage operations at that site are targeted for the early 2030s.

EU Innovation Fund Heat Auction distributes €400 M to 65 industrial heat decarbonisation projects

European Commission / Carbon Pulse  ·  22 May 2026  ·  [Europe]

The European Commission announced the selection of 65 projects under the first-ever EU-wide Innovation Fund Heat Auction, awarding €400 million from EU Emissions Trading System revenues. The auction targets innovative clean heat technologies for European industry. While this tranche does not directly fund CCS, it is structurally analogous to the Innovation Fund rounds that backed Northern Lights and other large-scale carbon capture projects — and signals continued EU commitment to channelling ETS revenue into hard-to-abate industrial sectors ahead of CBAM’s full activation in 2026.

UN General Assembly adopts landmark climate obligations resolution; Arab nations’ CCS amendment rejected

Carbon Brief  ·  22 May 2026  ·  [Global]

The UN General Assembly adopted a resolution affirming that states have binding legal obligations to address climate change, backed by 141 countries and opposed by 8 (including the US, Russia, Saudi Arabia, Iran, and Belarus), following the International Court of Justice advisory opinion. During negotiations, a bloc of largely Arab nations proposed an amendment that would have inserted positive language on the role of carbon capture and storage in climate solutions. The amendment was rejected. The episode highlights the contested geopolitics of CCS recognition at the UN level, with key hydrocarbon producers actively seeking its formal inclusion in multilateral climate frameworks.

LSB Industries secures pathway to 100% ownership of El Dorado CCS project, Arkansas

BusinessWire  ·  19 May 2026  ·  [North America]

US ammonia manufacturer LSB Industries agreed with Lapis Carbon Solutions to acquire full ownership of the El Dorado, Arkansas carbon capture and sequestration project (Project Blue). The facility is designed to permanently sequester 400,000–500,000 tCO₂/yr and produce 305,000–380,000 tpa of low-carbon ammonia, qualifying for US$85/t 45Q tax credits. Lapis will continue managing the EPA Class VI injection well permit application. Construction is targeted for completion by end-2026 or early 2027.

Global CCS Institute profiles Brevik CCS — world’s largest cement CO₂ capture plant

Global CCS Institute  ·  19 May 2026  ·  [Europe]

The Global CCS Institute published a detailed facility profile on Brevik CCS in Norway, operated by Heidelberg Materials. Commissioned in June 2025, Brevik captures CO₂ from cement production and ships it to Northern Lights’ offshore geological storage site in the Norwegian North Sea — making it the world’s largest cement plant CO₂ capture facility and a key anchor customer for Northern Lights Phase 1. The profile covers capture performance, logistics, and early-operations lessons.

Global CCS Institute  ·  18 May 2026  ·  [MENA]

The Emirate of Abu Dhabi published a General Policy on Carbon Capture, establishing a strategic and regulatory framework for CCS deployment in the emirate. The Global CCS Institute described the policy as “an important milestone in the evolution of carbon capture policy” in the MENA region. Abu Dhabi hosts the Al Reyadah CCS project (1 Mtpa, operational since 2016) and is positioning itself as a hub for regional and cross-border CCS development, including potential import storage for Asian industrial CO₂ emitters.
