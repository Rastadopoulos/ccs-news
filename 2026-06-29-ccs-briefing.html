Helms Geomarine secures multi-year Petronas CCS Solutions contract for offshore soil investigation across Malaysian CCS hubs

Offshore Energy · 26 Jun 2026 · [Australia & APAC]

Malaysian geotechnical specialist Helms Geomarine has won an initial three-year contract (with a two-year option) from Petronas CCS Solutions (PCCSS) covering offshore drilling and sampling, downhole CPTu testing, pile capacity and jack-up assessments, drivability analysis and geotechnical reporting for future Malaysian CCS infrastructure — storage facilities, injection wells, offshore platforms and subsea CO₂ pipelines. Work will be carried out from the newly commissioned DP2 and four-point mooring vessel Keyfield Itqan. The award is the first major supply-chain commitment from PCCSS following its 2025 corporate formation, and is timed against the Sarawak hub design work being led by the Mitsui-led eight-company consortium for the Japan–Malaysia CO₂ value chain. Material signal that Petronas is moving from MoU and FEED phase into ground-truthing the geology under multiple hub locations in parallel.

Flanders unveils €2 billion industrial decarbonisation package with €1.2 billion earmarked for CCS

Belga / Carbon Herald · 26 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The Flemish regional government announced its largest-ever industrial support mechanism: a €2 billion (~US$2.3 billion) ten-year envelope with €1.2 billion earmarked specifically for carbon capture and storage. Of that, €200 million is already allocated to the Kairos@C project (BASF / Air Liquide, Port of Antwerp), and €1 billion will be deployed via a competitive funding round launching in 2028 targeting hard-to-abate sectors — steel, chemicals and refining — across the Antwerp-Ghent industrial corridor. The remaining €800 million backs fuel-switching and electrification (electric boilers, industrial heat pumps). Minister-President Matthias Diependaele framed the package as economic-base preservation rather than climate compliance. The CCS earmark alone makes Flanders the third-largest single-region CCS public-funding commitment in Europe after the UK Track-2 award and Germany's €6 billion industrial decarbonisation initiative.

CCSA launches European CCUS National Associations Forum with nine founding country bodies

Carbon Herald · 26 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The Carbon Capture and Storage Association (CCSA) has launched the European CCUS National Associations Forum, an informal coordination platform bringing together nine national CCUS associations: Denmark's CO2 Hub Europe, France's Club CO2, Germany's Carbon Management Allianz and Deutsche Carbon Management Initiative, Norway's CCUS Innovation, Poland's CCUS Poland Association, Romania's Carbon Hub, Spain's PTECO2 and the Netherlands' Platform Carbon Management. The Forum's stated objective is to align national-capital regulatory positions with the Commission's policy direction in Brussels — closing the gap that has shown up between EU-level Net-Zero Industry Act provisions (50 Mt by 2030 storage target, Article 23 pro-rata obligations on oil-and-gas producers) and patchy Member State implementation. The Forum will also offer technical-data sharing into European nations without dedicated CCUS associations.

European Commission / Carbon Herald · 26 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

The European Commission has switched on the official platform for its Carbon Removals and Carbon Farming (CRCF) Buyers' Club — a voluntary public-private marketplace for CRCF-certified units announced at CRCF Days in May. Two tracks: permanent carbon removals (DAC, BECCS, biochar, mineralisation) and carbon farming. The Commission has set itself the target of making an initial set of public-money purchases of permanent removals by December 2026, with the Buyers' Club expected to anchor early offtake demand for the still-thin EU permanent-CDR market. SolarDuck and MARIN secure €3.2 million RVO subsidy for offshore floating power hub designed to serve CCS subsea infrastructure

Offshore Energy / SaurEnergy · 26 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK]

Dutch floating-solar developer SolarDuck and the Maritime Research Institute Netherlands (MARIN) have been awarded a €3.2 million grant by the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) under the "Steady Seas" programme to develop the Offshore Floating Power & Utility Hub (OFPH), a single-platform offshore solar solution intended to power and supply communications to remote subsea assets — including CCS injection facilities, monitoring systems and subsea tie-back projects. SolarDuck leads platform design and system integration; MARIN handles hydrodynamic analysis, mooring-and-motion simulation and basin testing. The work builds on the DEI+ Merganser demonstrator in the Dutch North Sea. If the cost curve works out it would reduce powered-monitoring lifecycle costs for offshore CCS storage sites — a category MMV cost line that hasn't had an obvious decarbonised-power answer to date.

Paris court orders TotalEnergies to extend climate-vigilance plan to Scope 3 emissions within six months

Euronews / Insurance Journal · 26 Jun 2026 · [Europe & UK / Global]

A Paris court ruled on 26 June that TotalEnergies must, within six months, revise its statutory climate-vigilance plan to formally assess and report on environmental risks generated by downstream consumer use of its fuels and natural gas — Scope 3 emissions — under France's 2017 duty-of-vigilance law. The court stopped short of ordering specific measures (no enforced cap on exploration, no binding emissions-reduction targets) but ordered €20,000 in legal costs payable to each plaintiff and scheduled a follow-up hearing for 21 January 2027. Significance: this is the first concrete application of the French duty-of-vigilance law to a major oil-and-gas producer's Scope 3 exposure, and is being read in Brussels as a marker for the Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) implementation track.
