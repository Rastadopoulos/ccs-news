Santos’ Moomba CCS project stores 2 million tonnes of CO₂

Santos  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [Australia / APAC — Projects]

The Moomba CCS facility in South Australia’s Cooper Basin has now permanently stored 2 million tonnes of CO₂e in just over 18 months of operation — equivalent to removing 826,000 cars from SA roads. The project has received more than 1.19 million Australian Carbon Credit Units (ACCUs) for CO₂e injected from commissioning through to 30 September 2025. The milestone was achieved despite two significant flooding events in the basin during the past year, demonstrating operational resilience at Australia’s largest onshore CCS facility (~1.7 Mtpa capacity).

INPEX takes first regulatory step for Bonaparte CCS project

Energy News Bulletin  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [Australia / APAC — Projects]

INPEX has resubmitted its environmental referral for the 10 Mtpa CO₂ storage project under the reformed EPBC Act, taking the first step down a re-aligned approvals pathway after withdrawing its earlier submission in January 2026 while legislative amendments were finalised. The Bonaparte Basin project sits ~250 km offshore Darwin in the Northern Territory; the joint venture is operated by INPEX (53%) with TotalEnergies CCS Australia (26%) and Woodside Energy (21%). FEED work is ongoing and the JV is targeting a Q2 2028 first injection.

Canada’s Pathways Alliance revises CCS capture goals in Ottawa-Alberta oilsands deal

Canada’s National Observer  ·  20 May 2026  ·  [North America — Projects / Policy]

Following the 15 May implementation agreement between PM Carney and Alberta Premier Smith, analysis reveals the Pathways Alliance has revised its CO₂ capture ambition from 22 Mtpa by 2030 to a near-term target of 10–12 Mtpa by 2030 and a longer-term goal of 16 Mtpa by 2035. The C$20 billion-plus project — the world’s largest CCS development — would link CO₂ capture plants north of Edmonton via a 400 km pipeline to an underground injection hub. The scaled-back targets reflect commercial and financing realities but the grand bargain gives the project formal go-ahead under federal support.
