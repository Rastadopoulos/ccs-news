Air Liquide commissions first industrial-scale Cryocap™ FG cement pilot at Holcim's CaptureLab, France

Air Liquide press release · 10 June 2026 · [EU/UK]

Air Liquide started up a Cryocap™ FG flue-gas pre-treatment pilot at Holcim's newly launched CaptureLab in Martres-Tolosane, France. The modular unit processes 3,000 Nm³/h of flue gas and targets >95% CO₂ recovery from cement kiln emissions, with the CaptureLab platform sized to capture 5–30 t CO₂/day pending scale-up to roughly 100× for commercial deployment.

Morecambe Net Zero Peak Cluster CCS project enters Assess Phase under NSTA carbon storage licence

Ocean News & Technology · 10 June 2026 · [EU/UK]

Spirit Energy's UK East Irish Sea cluster — pitched as the world's largest cement decarbonisation development — has progressed under licence CS010 toward CO₂ storage in the depleted North and South Morecambe gas fields. The project targets ~£1.8 bn of UK economic contribution by 2050 and decarbonisation of around 40% of UK cement and lime capacity, and is the first CCS development to secure investment from the National Wealth Fund.

Mitsubishi Electric and Finland's VTT reach milestone on electrochemical Direct Ocean Capture

Carbon Herald · 10 June 2026 · [Asia/Pacific–EU]

The partners completed core technology development for an acidification-based (acid-DOC) electrochemical system that introduces hydrogen ions into seawater to convert dissolved carbon back to gaseous CO₂ for recovery. With seawater CO₂ concentrations ~140× that of air (per unit volume), the team will now move from lab to a coastal demonstration and seek additional partners to push toward commercialisation.

Ocean Visions launches first-of-its-kind digital roadmap for seaweed-based decarbonisation pathways

Carbon Herald · 10 June 2026 · [Global]

The non-profit released a publicly available roadmap synthesising scientific, engineering, financial and policy readiness for seaweed-based products used in CDR and emissions reduction. Shaped by a May–June 2026 public comment period and an advisory board including Ling Cao (Xiamen University), Peter Green (Hatch Blue) and Rod Fujita (ex-EDF), the resource is intended to be iteratively updated as science and markets evolve.

LTi Vessco secures £4.5 m contract from Mitsubishi Heavy Industries for HyNet Padeswood CCS regenerator

Business News Wales · 10 June 2026 · [EU/UK]

Bridgend-based LTi Vessco will manufacture the ~56 m × 5 m regenerator column for the Padeswood cement CCS facility in North Wales, with MHI supplying the capture process technology and Worley acting as constructor.
