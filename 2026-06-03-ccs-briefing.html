UK Government sets 7th Carbon Budget at ~87% emissions cut, citing CCS-led industrial cluster build-out

UK Government / Mirage News · 2 Jun 2026 · [EU/UK]

The Department for Energy Security and Net Zero announced its proposed level for the 7th Carbon Budget — a science-led target of approximately an 87% emissions reduction in the period 2038–2042 (vs 1990) — and framed it as underpinned by over £90 billion of clean-energy private investment announced since July 2024, with Teesside CCS cluster build-out and Sizewell C cited as flagship enablers. OEUK summit releases DNV "Integrated and Resilient North Sea" report and Xodus cross-border CO₂ study — 18 Mtpa by 2030, 36 Mtpa by 2050

Offshore Energies UK / DNV / Xodus · 2 Jun 2026 · [EU/UK, transboundary]

At the closing day of the OEUK North Sea CCS & Hydrogen Summit in Edinburgh, DNV launched An Integrated and Resilient North Sea — examining CO₂ transport-and-storage and hydrogen transport across the UK, Norway, the Netherlands, Germany, Belgium and France — alongside an Xodus study Enabling Infrastructure for cross-border CO₂ transport, which finds the North Sea region will host the largest share of cross-border CO₂ flows, rising from 18 Mtpa in 2030 to 36 Mtpa by 2050. State of Carbon Dioxide Removal — 3rd Edition: 5 Gt CO₂/yr gap by 2050; novel CDR must scale faster than solar

Smith School of Enterprise & the Environment, University of Oxford / Mirage News / EurekAlert · 2 Jun 2026 · [Global]

The third edition of the State of CDR report, led by Oxford's Smith School with a five-organisation consortium, finds that national NDC pledges fall short of 1.5°C-aligned pathways by more than 5 Gt CO₂/yr by 2050 — a gap that would require novel carbon dioxide removal (DAC, BECCS, mineralisation, enhanced rock weathering) to grow at rates equal to or faster than the fastest historical clean-energy ramp-ups (solar PV, EVs). The world currently removes ~2.2 Gt CO₂/yr almost entirely through land-based methods, with novel-tech CDR at only ~0.1% of the total but growing at 40% p.a.; only about 20% of planned novel CDR capacity has actually been delivered to date, the authors warn, identifying 2026–2030 as the decisive deployment window. Useful direct hook to the CCS / capture chain via the BECCS, DAC and mineralisation pathway discussions.
