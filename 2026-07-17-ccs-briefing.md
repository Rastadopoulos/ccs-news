## Projects

- **Orcadian pitches a UK North Sea "Gigagrid" — offshore power with integrated CO₂ capture and reinjection to run a co-located data centre** — Orcadian Energy has begun the Assessment Phase for developing the Earlham and Orwell gas fields with a preferred concept of a ~300 MW offshore power station with integrated carbon capture (designed with GE Vernova and Norway's Capsol Technologies), fuelled by the fields' high-CO₂ gas, with the captured CO₂ reinjected into the reservoir and the low-carbon power feeding a co-located offshore AI data centre — the first phase of an "islanded" low-carbon offshore grid. Orcadian has farmed out 50% of the licence P2680 sub-area to the Marine Low Carbon Power Company (MLCP). Because the Earlham gas is too CO₂-rich for economic pipeline export, generate-and-reinject is framed as the optimal monetisation route. (Orcadian Energy RNS / Upstream, 16 Jul 2026) [link](https://www.investegate.co.uk/announcement/rns/orcadian-energy--orca/earlham-orwell-gas-development-concept-/9671317)

## Policy

- **Industry groups urge Baltic-bordering governments to clear the barriers to offshore CO₂ storage** — An alliance of industry stakeholders has called on the governments of Baltic Sea littoral states to address the obstacles still blocking offshore CO₂ storage in the basin, with the Helsinki Convention (HELCOM) singled out as the remaining legal barrier — unlike the London Protocol and OSPAR, which no longer bar sub-seabed storage elsewhere. An independent review of the legality, environmental impact and technical feasibility of CCS in the Baltic has been flagged. A transboundary/regulatory signal for a basin that several EU states are eyeing as shared storage. (QCIntel, 16 Jul 2026) [paywall] [link](https://www.qcintel.com/carbon/article/industry-groups-urge-action-to-enable-baltic-offshore-co2-storage-68906.html)
- **IOGP Europe warns the EU that "targets don't build projects" — enabling measures, not just storage targets, are what CCS needs** — IOGP Europe's Caterina De Matteis argued that the EU's Net-Zero Industry Act CO₂-injection targets will not deliver on their own without commercially viable frameworks, and that the forthcoming CO₂ markets and infrastructure legislative proposal must close the developer-vs-emitter commitment gap (storage is being built, but emitters are reluctant to commit). An industry-body positioning piece ahead of the EU's key CCS legislative package. (Carbon Herald, 16 Jul 2026) [link](https://carbonherald.com/iogps-caterina-de-matteis-europe-co2-storage-targets/)

## Technology

- **Study maps calcium-looping direct air capture as a route to cut cement-manufacturing emissions** — A peer-reviewed analysis in *Joule* assesses calcium-looping direct air capture for reducing cement-manufacturing emissions, exploiting the synergy between the CaCO₃/CaO sorbent chemistry of DAC and the cement kiln — a pathway toward net-negative cement by cycling sorbent through the DAC unit before calcination. Adds to a growing body of work (a companion Cell *Chem Circularity* LCA appeared in June) on DAC–cement integration. (ScienceDirect / Joule, 16 Jul 2026) [link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2542435126002655)
- **Bioforcetech turns sewage sludge into carbon-negative concrete filler — and destroys PFAS in the process** — Californian firm Bioforcetech's "OurCarbon" process converts municipal sewage sludge into a CO₂-negative concrete ingredient while breaking down "forever chemicals" (PFAS), diverting organics from methane-emitting landfill. The company won a US$310,000 Bridge Carbontech grant from Columbia University's Carbontech Development Initiative to build a dosing system letting existing concrete batch plants inject the material automatically. A modest but genuine CO₂-utilisation / durable-storage catch. (Carbon Herald, 16 Jul 2026) [link](https://carbonherald.com/innovative-building-materials-from-sewage-sludge-lock-away-co2-for-centuries/)
